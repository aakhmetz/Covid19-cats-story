COVID-19 in a Cat: Spain

By Scott Weese on May 8, 2020
Posted in Cats

Pretty soon, I’ll stop writing about single reports of cats identified as infected with SARS-CoV-2. It’s not surprising  and sporadic case reports don’t provide much new information. However, I’ll give the quick rundown of the latest case from Spain. It’s only reported in news articles, as far as I can find, so we have to be a bit wary of the info. However, it’s not overly surprising.

As expected, the cat lived in a household where people were infected. The cat developed respiratory difficulties, and was ultimately euthanized. Low levels of SARS-CoV-2 were found in its nasal passages and an abdominal lymph node. The cat also had hypertrophic cardiomyopathy, a potentially serious heart disease that can cause death.

This case raises concern about a severe COVID-19 infection in the cat, different than the milder cases that have been previously reported. However, the cat’s underlying health issues could have accounted for death, so it’s more likely that the cat had an incidental infection rather than a fatal COVID-19 infection.

Regardless, my assumption has been that we’re bound to see the odd serious disease in cats. I get some calls about suspicious cases but we haven’t been able to test too many. Occasional serious disease wouldn’t be surprising.  Most cats probably get no disease or mild disease (like people) if they get infected but it’s reasonable to expect that a subset (especially those with underlying health problems) could get more serious disease. However, most cats exposed to infected people likely don’t get infected. Of those that get infected, probably few get sick and serious complications of infection are probably  rare.

As per all my other discussions, the key is social distancing pets and the risk that a pet would bring this virus into the household is very low.
